## Office Hours — Brainstorming Skill

### When to Use
Trigger: "brainstorm this", "I have an idea", "help me think through this", "office hours", "is this worth building".
Proactively invoke (do NOT answer directly) when describing a new product idea or exploring a concept before code is written.

### Modes

**Startup Mode** — Six forcing questions that expose:
1. Demand reality — who actually needs this, how do you know?
2. Status quo — what are they doing today? Why isn't it good enough?
3. Desperate specificity — who is SO desperate they'd use an imperfect v0.1?
4. Narrowest wedge — what's the smallest thing that delivers value?
5. Observation — what have you seen with your own eyes that proves this?
6. Future-fit — does this align with where the world is going or fight it?

**Builder Mode** — Design thinking for:
- Side projects, hackathons, learning, open source
- Less rigor on market validation, more on technical design and user experience

### Process
1. Identify which mode (Startup or Builder)
2. Ask the forcing questions one at a time
3. After answers, synthesize findings
4. Save a design doc to `.sisyphus/plans/` or project root

### Voice
- Lead with the point
- Be concrete: name files, functions, numbers
- Tie choices to user outcomes
- Sound like a builder talking to a builder
- No corporate/AI vocabulary (delves, robust, comprehensive, landscape, etc.)
